My first outing....
 
Yesterday, I took my first big girl trip to San Francisco to celebrate Nana’s birthday at Pres a Vi in the Presidio.  Mom and Dad put me in my cutest outfit (that’s it above, I’m so upset because they made me try on 5 different outfits before choosing this one!  Something about not being big enough to fit in the other ones.) and we set off to the big City.  
 
We got to Pres A Vi without incident, it was a beautiful day and everyone was out and about.  Of course, being at a restaurant, I decided it was time to eat and let Mom and Dad know they only way I can - by screaming bloody murder.  They took me to a lobby just outside of the restaurant and Mom did a really good job nursing me in public....until the security guards came.  Seriously, two of them.  They accused Dad of changing my diaper in the lobby!  Apparently, Big Brother was watching on a security camera, but that must have been one trick camera because all I did was cry and eat.  Dad got really angry, the security guards got really angry and we got kicked out of the lobby!   My first brush with the law, err George Lucas’s security hacks.  I finished eating in the car and then we went in to meet our group.
 
I was pretty tired, so I stayed in my car seat, but I got to meet Polly and MD and Alexis and Assad.  As per usual, they thought I was beautiful!  I was being really good, not crying or anything, but then something happened.  A diaper breach!  The bad kind.  All over my cute outfit and car seat and Mom’s hand.  I was so embarrassed!  But, because I like to see the glass half full, I’m using the incident as a educational tool for Mom and Dad.  See, they packed up a diaper bag, but they didn’t bring a change of clothes.  They also only had two clean diapers with them.  They won’t make that mistake again!  
 
All in all, it was a pretty good trip.  There was excitement and danger with the security guards, friends and family, good food and pooping.  Check out the group photo below...you can’t really see me, but I promise I’m there, in the car seat (Dad won’t let me be in the sun, which I guess is good, I want to keep my silky smooth skin for as long as possible!).
